Purpose of Austin Animal Center Analysis

The purpose of this analysis is to review data on animals in the Austin Animal Center to better understand the inventory of the animals and to create a model that predicts the outcome whether that is:

  • Adoption
  • Transfer
  • Return to Owner
  • Died

    • Austin Animal Center Backgroup

    The Austin Animal Center is the largest no-kill animal shelter in the United States and houses 18,000 animals each year. The shelter is an open intake facility where all lost or surrendered animals are accepted. As the city of Austin grows the number of animal intakes will increase making the burden on the shelter greater.

    The ability of the shelter to remain no-kill is dependent on having available room to house all of the animals and makes understanding the outcome of the animals and the success of the adoption program incential.

    Visual Analysis

    One of the most important features to the animal shelter as far as space is concerned is the length of stay of the animals and the associated characteristics of the animal that impact the length of stay. The below visual shows the average length of stay by different characteristics and can be filtered by animal type and outcome.

    The number of intakes and outgoing animals trend together across all months indicating that the shelter is able to keep up with the demand of the number of animals. This visual can be filtered by animal type and outcome type.

    There is an increase in the number of animals during the spring and summer months which is due to a fluctuation in the number of cats possibly a higher number of kittens during this timeframe.

    The number of birds also has a peak in intakes and outcomes during the spring months possibly due to baby birds being displaced.

    The number of dog intakes and outgoing animals is much more consistent throughout the year.

    This information can be helpful in determining when special events, marketing or contacting with transfering partners to assist in the placement for animals during higher intact months.

    The number of spay and nueter surgeries is important cost factor for the shelter to consider each year for budgeting reasons.

    Strays make up the largest intake type and are the largest number in all outcome types except for Euthanasia which wildlife holds the largest number.

    These visuals can be filtered by outcome type and animal type.
